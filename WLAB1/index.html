Raleigh, North Carolina, USA

History

Known as the city of oaks, Raleigh is the capital city of the U.S. state of North Carolina. The city is named after Sir Walter Raleigh who established Roanoke colony in present-day Dare county. Raleigh was chosen as the site of North Carolina's capital in 1788 an was officially established in 1792. Raleigh is an early example in the United States of a planned city. The establishment of the Research Triangle Park (RTP) in 1959 help created thousands of jobs. Raleigh had become one of the fastest-growing cities in the United States by the early 21st century.

Things to do in Raleigh

  1. Access NC's fresh and locally grown produce at the popular state farmers market
  2. See exhibits on NC's geology, flora and fauna at the NC Museum of Natural Sciences
  3. Boating and other outdoor activities at the NC Pullen Park
